# Cast Bible — Proposals Vol. 1

> Authored by Claude (Sonnet 4.6) on commission from Talon. This is a **proposal document for design review** — not merged canon. Nothing here is authoritative until Talon says so. The intent is to give the cast system more surface area to play against: more role diversity, more dramatic arc potential, more of the simulation's existing machinery (stress, willpower, social mask, inhibitions, eavesdrop, favor economy, threshold spaces, emotional topography) engaged with characters who are specifically designed to stress those systems in interesting ways.
>
> Read alongside: `cast-bible.md`, `action-gating.md`, `new-systems-ideas.md`, `new-systems-ideas-vol2.md`, `world-bible.md`, `dialog-bible.md`.

---

## Expansion goals

The existing ten archetypes cover the emotional poles well. The Vent and The Hermit anchor opposite ends of the social-drive spectrum. The Climber and the Cynic bracket ambition. The Affair and The Recovering give the simulation its long-burn dramatic arcs. What the current catalog is lighter on is the *middle* — the people who aren't defined by a single dominant force but by the gap between what they are and what the role asks of them. The proposals below try to fill that middle and the edges it doesn't have yet: the institutionally invisible, the perpetually liminal, the quietly wronged, the structurally powerful-without-authority.

The secondary goal is coverage of the building's vertical layers. The current cast implicitly lives on the first floor. The basement production floor and the top-floor exec suite each have their own social physics. A cast that spans all three floors creates more inter-floor friction events, more AbsenceRippleSystem interactions, more instances of the elevator as a social microcosm.

## Part I: Existing Role Expansions

These proposals do not change who the archetypes are. They add ECS surface area, behavioral hooks, and relationship patterns that the existing archetype descriptions imply but don't yet specify.

---

### The Vent *(Donna)*

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Vent's chronically elevated Belonging and Irritation drives create a character who is simultaneously hungry for connection and structurally bad at maintaining it, because sustained irritation venting depresses Trust in the listener over time. The simulation should model this feedback loop: Donna wants to connect, connects by venting, mildly erodes the relationship she's trying to build, vents harder when she senses the erosion. What the archetype doesn't yet specify is the *recovery* behavior — after a vent session that went too far, The Vent often overcorrects with warmth, bringing food, performing cheerfulness, doing a visible favor. This recovery loop is an excellent FavorEconomySystem hook and should be modeled as part of the archetype's post-vent behavioral sequence.

**Social dynamics.** The Vent is the primary gossip node in any cast. The RumorSystem should flag Vent-type NPCs as high-throughput information nodes: they transfer facts at high rate but also introduce distortion, because their emotional state inflects what they pass on. A fact heard through a Vent NPC should carry elevated confidence for emotional content ("she was furious") and reduced confidence for factual content ("I think the meeting was about a merger"). The dialog bible's calcification mechanism means The Vent's tics form fastest around complaint and share contexts — whoever Donna vents to most becomes the NPC who can finish her sentences, which is itself a social fact that changes how others read that pair.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The Vent archetype has an unwritten ceiling moment: the day the vent doesn't land. The NPC they've been venting to for six months is at their own breaking point. The Vent tries to unload and the listener, for the first time, either closes down or snaps. This is a MaskSlip event on the *listener*, not The Vent — generated by The Vent's normal behavior triggering someone else's willpower collapse. The Vent walks away confused and slightly wounded. The listener walks away with shame and irritation at having shown themselves. Both have a relationship to resolve.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `Belonging.Baseline: [62, 78]`, `Irritation.Baseline: [60, 75]`. A `publicEmotion` inhibition at low-to-mid strength (strength range `[10, 30]`) — The Vent does not suppress emotion, but there is still a threshold before genuine breakdown. The Vent archetype specifically benefits from a high `FavorEconomySystem.GiveFrequency` after venting events — make it a standard behavioral beat.

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I'm just being real with you." Actual interior: craves validation, deeply afraid of being alone in the building in any emotional sense, has confused venting-as-intimacy with actual intimacy for so long that genuine quiet closeness feels threatening.

### The Hermit *(Greg)*

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Hermit's loneliness drive is chronically elevated but the action it generates is avoidance, not approach — this is the approach-avoidance inversion from the action-gating bible in its purest form. What should be specified more concretely: Greg's IT closet is both his refuge space (mask maintenance cost near zero there) and his trap (he never builds the social capital to change his conditions). The FlowStateSystem makes Greg the single NPC most likely to achieve deep flow — his workspace is acoustically isolated, he controls access, his proximity-event rate is low. His work output, when measured, should be quietly excellent. The simulation should make this readable to the player.

**Social dynamics.** Greg has an unusual relationship to the RumorSystem: he overhears things through the server-room wall (EavesdropSystem flagged with `source: overheard`, high confidence, MustSuppressFlag active for most) without anyone knowing he overhears them. Over time, Greg's KnowledgeComponent accumulates sensitive information he never sought and never uses. This makes him the most informationally rich NPC in the cast and the one least likely to weaponize it — which is its own kind of power and its own kind of burden. The SecretWeightSystem's IrritabilityAccumulator should be tuned for Greg because he's been holding things a long time.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** Greg as the emergency pivot. During a crisis — deadline, illness wave, equipment failure — Greg is the only person who can fix the specific thing. He has to emerge. He doesn't want to. He does it anyway. The crisis resolves. Everyone is grateful. The next day, Greg is back in the server room and people have already somewhat forgotten. This should be a narrative event hook: `EmergentExpertise`, fired when an NPC who's been socially marginalized becomes structurally essential. The aftermath — the forgetting — is as important as the moment.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `Exposure.Baseline: [65, 80]` (proposed ninth drive), `Loneliness.Baseline: [60, 72]`, `Trust.Baseline: [25, 40]`. `vulnerability` inhibition `[70, 90]`, `physicalIntimacy` inhibition `[60, 80]`. Refuge-space designation for IT closet should specify Greg as an archetype beneficiary — drive recovery rate 1.5× when in that room. `FlowState.EntryThresholdMultiplier: 0.7` (enters flow more easily than population average).

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I prefer machines." Actual interior: acute awareness of every social interaction in his peripheral field; a person who notices everything and processes nothing outward; genuinely content in isolation until the ContentmentFacade cracks along the seam of loneliness.

### The Climber

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Climber's deceptively high agreeableness is a performance, and the simulation should model the cost of that performance explicitly. They are the archetype with the highest MaskStrength baseline — they've trained themselves not to emit — but this means their mask is also the most expensive to maintain. The BodyLanguageReadSystem note about The Climber is exactly right: other high-empathy NPCs find their signal "faintly uncanny." This should translate into a specific social mechanic: very high-empathy NPCs who interact with The Climber regularly develop elevated Suspicion toward them over time, not from any specific incident but from the cumulative wrongness of someone who never reads as distressed. The tells are too clean.

**Social dynamics.** The Climber is the primary FavorEconomySystem actor. Their ledger is meticulous. The relationship hook this creates with the Confidant relationship pattern: The Climber has likely given information or favors to multiple NPCs in ways that created knowledge asymmetry — those NPCs know things because The Climber told them, which means The Climber can control the narrative. This is a distinct play from the Old Hand's passive equity accumulation and should be modeled as higher-velocity, more transactional, ultimately more fragile.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The Climber's moment of genuine emotion — the one that breaks through the mask not because willpower is depleted but because something mattered more than the climb. The simulation should make this rare and specific: not the mask-slip of exhaustion but the mask-slip of caring. The aftermath is the interesting part. They recover. They file the moment away. But it was seen, and the NPC who saw it has a new piece of KnowledgeComponent that is different from all the others.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `Status.Baseline: [72, 85]`, `publicEmotion` inhibition `[60, 75]`, `vulnerability` inhibition `[55, 70]`. `FavorEconomySystem.TrackingAccuracy: 0.95` (archetype parameter — Climbers track favors with near-perfect precision). `MaskStrength.BaselineMultiplier: 1.3` (their mask is professionally calibrated). `ObservableSignalComponent.SignalReliability: 0.3` — their emitted signals are the least accurate representation of their internal state in the cast.

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I just work hard and care about outcomes." Actual interior: genuine hunger for significance that exists independently of the tactics used to get there; a person who started performing ambition and no longer knows whether they're performing.

### The Cynic

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Cynic is the archetype most resistant to StressSystem accumulation — they don't suppress irritation, so it doesn't compound; they don't perform agreeableness, so they don't pay mask maintenance cost. This should make them the emotionally cheapest archetype to run, which produces a specific behavior: they have surplus. Surplus attention, surplus bluntness, surplus capacity to notice things others are too depleted to catch. The Cynic is the canary. When the Cynic seems tired, something structural has gone very wrong.

**Social dynamics.** The Cynic and The True Believer (proposed new archetype) are natural foils — a relationship dynamic worth seeding explicitly. The Cynic has seen the institution's face too many times; The True Believer hasn't looked. Their interactions are the office's running ideological argument, except it's conducted through the mundane register of daily friction: the eye roll at the mandatory training, the cheerful counterargument about the new process, the slow wearing at each other that neither of them wants to stop.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The Cynic's crisis of faith — the moment when something in the office is bad enough that even cynical distance can't protect them from caring. This fires rarely. When it does, it should be a high-noteworthiness NarrativeEvent: the NPC who never breaks is broken. The rest of the office doesn't know what to do with it. The Newbie especially is alarmed — they were using the Cynic as a north star.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `StressSystem.ChronicAccumulationRate: 0.6` (lower than population average — cynicism as genuine stress buffer). `publicEmotion` inhibition `[5, 20]` — the Cynic has almost no suppression on emotional expression. `Belonging.Baseline: [35, 52]` — lower than average; they've stopped needing it, mostly. `MaskStrength.DecayRate: 0.6` — mask costs them less because they're not performing much.

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I'm past it." Actual interior: not past it. Has just reorganized caring around a narrower set of things that would be embarrassing to admit to.

### The Newbie

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Newbie's IntegrationStateComponent should interact with the FlowStateSystem in a specific way: they can't achieve flow because they answer every question and interrupt every interruption. They don't know they're doing this. The system should not punish them mechanically for it — the work output is acceptable, just never excellent. What it should model is the slow discovery: at some point, after enough interruptions-of-others and enough un-asked-for-help, the Newbie starts to notice that the people around them are slightly more closed than they were. They don't know why. They try harder. This makes it worse.

**Social dynamics.** The Newbie is the social reveal mechanic for the cast. They ask questions nobody else will ask. They violate norms nobody else will violate. They walk toward Cubicle 12. They ask about Mark. Each violation is a window for the player into the suppressed history of the floor. This should be specified as an archetype property: `KnowledgeBarrierBreakProbability` — the baseline likelihood per sim-day that a Newbie-type NPC generates an event where they unknowingly breach a suppressed social norm.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The Newbie's first genuinely good idea. Not just helpful — actually smart, actually original. The meeting where they say something and the room goes quiet in a different way. The social politics of that moment — who acknowledges it, who doesn't, whether The Climber moves to adopt it before the Newbie has finished the sentence — is the whole office in miniature.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `Agreeableness: [+1, +2]`, `Conscientiousness: [-1, +0]` (trying hard, not yet efficient). `Belonging.Baseline: [65, 80]` (chronically hungry for acceptance). `FlowState.InterruptionRate: 1.8×` standard — they generate more interruptions than average, not from malice. `IntegrationStateComponent.KnowledgeBarrierBreakProbability: 0.15` per sim-day (roughly once a week they walk into something).

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I'm just eager to help." Actual interior: scared of being the person who doesn't belong, performing competence they're still building, using helpfulness as an apology for existing.

### The Old Hand

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Old Hand is the archetype with the highest natural FavorLedger balance — they give without tracking, which is why people do things for them without being asked. The simulation should model this as a slow institutional gravity: the Old Hand is the NPC others drift toward in a crisis without being able to explain why. Their schedule should include unexpected informal mentoring moments — not the Mentor relationship pattern (which is formal and asymmetric) but the Old Hand version, which is more like a passing comment in a hallway that turns out to be exactly the thing someone needed.

**Social dynamics.** The Old Hand has the most developed KnowledgeComponent in the cast. They remember things that predate the current carpet. They know about Mark in a way that is different from knowing about Mark — they were there. The SecretWeightSystem's CarryingCost for Old Hand-type NPCs who hold the Cubicle 12 fact should be lower than for others, not because they care less but because they've metabolized it over time. The weight is normalized. It no longer costs them mask energy. It's just a room that was once something else.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The Old Hand leaving. Voluntary or otherwise. The AbsenceRippleSystem and the EmotionalTopographySystem together describe what happens to a floor when the person who contained the institutional memory is gone. Their desk is cleared. Their parking spot is suddenly available. The building doesn't know what it's lost yet. It will figure it out over the next several weeks, through small failures, through the absence of the sentence that would have been said.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `Neuroticism: [-2, -1]` (very stable). `Trust` and `Suspicion` both mid-range but with high confidence — they know exactly who to trust and why, and they're right. `FavorEconomySystem.GiveFrequency: 1.5×`, `FavorEconomySystem.TrackingAccuracy: 0.2` (gives freely, doesn't track). `SecretWeightSystem.NormalizationMultiplier: 0.4` for long-held facts — the Old Hand's carrying cost diminishes over game-time for facts held since early in the save.

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I've seen this before." Actual interior: occasionally overwhelmed by how much they've seen; a specific grief around the version of this place that no longer exists.

### The Affair

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Affair archetype has the most complex dialog register in the cast — code-switching between hidden and public modes, per-listener fragment tracking. What should be specified: the behavioral cost of code-switching. Every time The Affair transitions between modes (from a conversation with the affair partner to a conversation with a neutral NPC, or from private to observed context), there is a brief lag where the wrong register almost surfaces. This lag is the tell. High-empathy NPCs in proximity should receive a small Suspicion bump from these lag events, even without a specific content flag.

**Social dynamics.** The Affair's relationship to The Recovering is worth seeding. The Recovering, by definition, has experience hiding something that's slowly destroying them. They may recognize the specific quality of The Affair's code-switching without knowing its content — a solidarity born of shared technique. This is the basis for a Confidant relationship pattern: they know each other's tells because they share them.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The affair ending. Not through discovery — through the simple, private collapse of something that was costing too much. The affair partner leaves the company, or The Affair decides to stop, or the thing just bleeds out. The aftermath: the Exposure drive doesn't know to come down. The mask habits that were trained to hide the affair are still running, protecting nothing. The NPC looks guilty without having anything to be guilty about. People notice. They assume the wrong thing.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `Exposure.Baseline: [65, 78]` (proposed ninth drive — chronically elevated for this archetype). `infidelity` inhibition `[10, 30]` (low strength, the inhibition is failing). `vulnerability` inhibition `[72, 88]` (cannot afford to be seen). `DialogSystem.RegisterCodeSwitchLatencyTicks: [3, 8]` — brief window where the previous register can contaminate the current one. Suspicion bump of `[3, 7]` points for high-empathy NPCs who observe a register lag event.

**Mask layers.** Projects: "Everything is fine." Actual interior: everything is not fine; they know it; they can't stop; the cost they're paying is now just part of the overhead of being themselves.

### The Recovering

**Additional behavioral traits.** The SubstanceDriftSystem's `SobrietyStreakComponent` should interact with the FavorEconomySystem in a specific way: The Recovering gives generously as a moral project, which means their FavorLedger is chronically positive and often exploited by NPCs who have noticed this pattern without naming it. The Climber has noticed. The StrainAccumulator for The Recovering should have a specific input from favor-exploitation events — being used is a stress that the archetype is specifically vulnerable to, because the generosity is meant to mean something and when it doesn't, the cost lands on the sobriety math.

**Social dynamics.** The Recovering's relationship to The Affair (proposed above) is one hook. A second: The Recovering and The Old Hand. The Old Hand knows. They've never said. They've just been quietly present in the ways that count. This is a deep-background relationship that has no explicit event history but manifests as the highest Trust score The Recovering has in the cast. It's not transactional. It's just weight-bearing.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The relapse. Not as drama-for-drama's sake but as the mechanical output of the StrainAccumulator crossing a threshold after a specific week. The player has been watching the accumulation. The moment comes. What happens next is what the archetype is built for: the morning after, the reset, the deciding whether to tell anyone. The sobriety counter in SobrietyStreakComponent goes back to zero. The archetype carries that number.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `SobrietyStreakComponent.StrainInputFromFavorExploitation: [4, 9]` per detected exploitation event. `Neuroticism: [+1, +2]` (volatile under pressure). `Affection.Baseline: [62, 75]` — they want connection; recovery is partly social. `vulnerability` inhibition `[65, 80]` but with `awareness: hidden` — they cannot see that they're hiding.

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I'm doing the work." Actual interior: doing the work, but the work is harder than anyone watching them could know from outside; the cheerfulness is expensive; the generosity is a prayer.

### The Founder's Nephew

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Founder's Nephew has an inverted relationship to the TemporalAnxietySystem — Monday mornings, end-of-quarter, performance reviews land differently for them because none of the stakes are real. The simulation should model the absence of temporal anxiety as its own behavioral signature: the NPC who never checks the clock differently near a deadline, whose baseline doesn't shift at end-of-quarter, who fills out the mandatory training module at 9:40am on the deadline day and considers this acceptable. The affectless relationship to institutional time is what other NPCs read and find intolerable.

**Social dynamics.** The Founder's Nephew is the cast's lightning rod for ambient resentment. NPCs who have performed extremely well and received minimal recognition accumulate Irritation drive specifically in proximity to the Nephew. This should be modeled explicitly: a small ambient Irritation increment for high-Conscientiousness, high-effort NPCs who occupy awareness range with a Founder's Nephew-type NPC for extended periods. The resentment is not from specific events. It's from the visible asymmetry.

**Untapped dramatic potential.** The Nephew surprising everyone. Not through competence — through a moment of unexpected empathy or loyalty that the cast didn't see coming. It should be small and uncharacteristic and possibly not even acknowledged by the Nephew themselves. The player notices. It doesn't change the fundamental dynamic. But it's there.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `TemporalAnxietySystem.AnticipationModifier: 0.1×` (nearly unaffected by calendar events). `Status.Baseline: [35, 50]` — their actual social respect is low even if their job security is high. This is the status inversion the cast bible describes. `Conscientiousness: [-2, -1]`. Ambient Irritation increment for adjacent high-Conscientiousness NPCs: `[2, 5]` per sim-hour in shared awareness range.

**Mask layers.** Projects: "I'm doing fine." Actual interior: not actually sure what they're supposed to be doing or whether anyone would tell them if they were failing; the security is real but so is the ambient awareness that it's the only thing about them that is.

### The Crush

**Additional behavioral traits.** The Crush archetype is the most passive in terms of its own drive profile — their state is ordinary; it's *another NPC's* state that defines them. What should be specified: The Crush has no special knowledge of being crushed-upon. But they have anomalous behavioral signals that they can't explain — they find their awareness pulled toward the pursuing NPC during lulls, not out of attraction but out of some dimly perceived asymmetry in how that NPC relates to them. The ObservableSignalComponent from the crusher, over time, is slightly off in a way that reads as attention. The Crush's Suspicion toward the crusher should rise very slowly — not accusatory, just a vague not-sure-what-this-is.

**Social dynamics.** The Crush generates interesting secondary dynamics when the pursuing NPC is in a relationship pattern with a *third* NPC. The Old Flame, for example, watching someone develop a crush on their complicated ex. The Affair, whose pursuit of their secret partner is already loaded, now also being crushed-upon by someone who doesn't know. These second-order effects should be easy to generate from the relationship seeder — the Crush archetype should occasionally be seeded into existing relationship-adjacent NPCs.

**Proposed ECS additions.** `Trust.Baseline` toward the pursuing NPC: starts normal, develops slight Suspicion increment per sim-day that the pursuing NPC's observable signals read as elevated. Cap at `[15, 25]` additional Suspicion — not enough to produce avoidance, enough to produce awareness. The Crush's own drive states should remain ordinary except for a small `Belonging` uncertainty increment around the pursuing NPC — something registers as off-balance, in an interesting way.

## Part II: Proposed New Characters

Each character below is a design illustration — a way of showing how a new archetype combination could be instantiated. They are not mandated roster members; the generator should produce its own versions. Names are placeholders. The archetypes they illustrate are the part worth keeping.

### Nora
**Role/Title.** Mid-level manager, first floor. Has direct reports. Is technically responsible for their output.

**Archetype tag.** The Placeholder.

**Physical/aesthetic impression.** Average height, average build, nothing visually distinctive. Her dominant color is the kind of beige that is only a shade away from the walls. She carries a folder she never opens. The folder may be empty. Nobody has ever asked.

**Surface personality.** Professionally pleasant. Uses meeting language outside of meetings. Says "let's circle back" and "I'll take that offline" and means both of them. Nods a lot. Does not initiate but does not block.

**Hidden interior.** Nora knows she shouldn't be managing people. She knew it the week she was given the title. She's been managing the knowledge of this fact for four years. Every meeting is a performance of authority she doesn't feel, and the performance is getting more expensive not less because the gap between her title and her actual influence has only grown as the people around her have grown past her.

**Core drive profile.** Status drive elevated but inverted — it's not ambition, it's the terror of status *loss*. Belonging drive chronically elevated. Fear elevated. Irritation suppressed at high willpower cost because expressing it would require owning something.

**Willpower profile.** Baseline medium-high (65–75). Eroded by any situation that requires an actual decision with actual consequences. Restored by tasks with no social component — completing a spreadsheet, processing an administrative request.

**Social web.** Orbits The Climber (who has learned to route around her authority for almost everything). Relies on Sandy (see below) for what actually gets done. The Newbie initially reports to her and gradually figures out that the information they need comes from elsewhere. Is vaguely threatened by The Old Hand, who never says anything but who everyone treats as more authoritative.

**Signature behavior.** Calls a meeting about something that doesn't require a meeting. In the meeting, facilitates without deciding. Assigns follow-up items to other people. Files the meeting as resolved. The MeetingSystem should model her meetings as having high CaptiveComponent duration and low information-transfer output.

**Narrative event hooks.** OverdueTask (her reports' tasks, because she hasn't created clarity on priorities), MaskSlip (in one-on-one contexts when she can't perform authority — the agreeableness drops and the fear surfaces), LeftRoomAbruptly (after a meeting where someone expected her to make a call she didn't make).

**Story arc potential.** The arc of someone finally deciding whether to admit to the role they actually are — and whether the admission is liberating or ending.

### Dale
**Role/Title.** Senior administrative staff, first floor. No formal title anyone can name. Been here longer than the coffee maker.

**Archetype tag.** The Fixture.

**Physical/aesthetic impression.** Stocky, short, moves slowly and with confidence. Always wears the same rotating set of plaid shirts. Has a mug that is so specific to him it has been called by his name — "Can you grab Dale?" — without anyone realizing the implication. The mug has been through a dishwasher approximately once.

**Surface personality.** Low-affect, not unfriendly. Efficient at the things he's efficient at. Politely impervious to the things he's not efficient at, which is most new things. Has opinions about the correct way to do things that he formed in 1997 and has not revisited.

**Hidden interior.** Dale is not unaware that the world has changed and that he hasn't changed with it. He is aware and he has made a decision. The decision is: this is the floor I've been standing on for fifteen years and it's still here and so am I. This is not bitterness. It is a different kind of faith — in duration, in place, in the simple fact of continuing.

**Core drive profile.** Belonging moderate, Loneliness low (for the archetype) — he has enough connection. Status drive very low. Trust drive specific and calibrated — he trusts the people he's decided to trust and not the others and this has not changed. Irritation drive slow to rise, slow to fall.

**Willpower profile.** Baseline high (75–85). Erodes mainly from sustained change pressure — new processes, new technology, new managers who want to optimize something that doesn't need optimizing. Restored by routine.

**Social web.** Deep relationship with The Old Hand (they were here at the same time, they remember the same things). Territorial relationship with his desk, his mug, his filing cabinet (TerritorialClaimComponent maxed on all three). Ambient suspicion from The Climber, who has tried to route around Dale twice and discovered that Dale's informal authority over certain administrative processes is real and non-negotiable.

**Signature behavior.** Fixes the thing that's been broken for three weeks in four minutes after everyone else has given up. Doesn't mention it. Goes back to his desk.

**Narrative event hooks.** EmotionalTopographySystem moments (Dale has personal associations with almost every named anchor on the floor), FavorEconomy credit calls from NPCs who remember when he helped them, TerritorialViolation events from new hires who don't know the geography.

**Story arc potential.** The arc of someone who has committed to duration — what they protect by staying, what they lose, and whether the floor would still be the floor without them.

### Priya
**Role/Title.** Administrative assistant / receptionist. Graduate degree in a field she doesn't discuss.

**Archetype tag.** The Overqualified.

**Physical/aesthetic impression.** Tall, elegant posture, slightly over-dressed for the role in a way that reads as either pride or refusal. Always has something to read at the desk during slow moments — not a magazine, a book with a cover she sometimes turns face-down. Her desk is visually clean in a way that communicates a relationship to the space that is temporary.

**Surface personality.** Warmly competent, efficient, reliable, slightly formal. Does exactly what is required with no visible resentment. The resentment is in the vocabulary register — she speaks to everyone with the same faint precision, including the people who don't notice and the people who do.

**Hidden interior.** She told herself this was temporary. It's been two years. The temporary is starting to look structural. The gap between what she knows she can do and what she is doing shows up as a specific low-grade hum in her internal state — not explosive, not dramatic, just permanently present. She's stopped talking about what she studied. She started in month six.

**Core drive profile.** Status drive elevated (frustrated, not expressed). Loneliness drive elevated (she doesn't fit any of the social groups, is above some in ways she can't say, below others in ways that are formal). Affection drive moderate — she wants to be known. Fear drive specifically around being permanently defined by this role.

**Willpower profile.** Baseline medium (60–70). Eroded by being asked to do things her qualification makes trivial. Paradoxically also eroded by being asked to do things above her role without acknowledgment — the double bind is exhausting. Restored by any task that uses the actual capacity she has.

**Social web.** Sandy (proposed below) is her closest relationship — administrative proximity, mutual reliability, no status competition between them. The Climber has identified her as potentially useful and has been cultivating a relationship she finds slightly transparent. The Cynic has noticed her and said, once, exactly what they think her situation is. She didn't respond. She's thought about it every day since.

**Signature behavior.** When given a task her degree over-qualifies her for, completes it perfectly and gives it back with one additional observation that suggests the larger context nobody asked her about. The observation is correct. It is noted and not acted on. She doesn't offer it twice.

**Narrative event hooks.** MaskSlip (when something condescending happens at the edge of what she can absorb), OverdueTask on *her behalf* (things being assigned to her that should be assigned to someone more capable), eventual StatusRecalibration if she is ever given something that matches what she actually is.

**Story arc potential.** The arc of staying or leaving — and the question of which one is giving up.

### Marcus
**Role/Title.** HR generalist / compliance officer. First floor.

**Archetype tag.** The True Believer.

**Physical/aesthetic impression.** Medium build, always in business casual, always slightly more formal than the floor. Carries a black binder. The binder has dividers. The dividers are labeled. He is the only person in the building with a functional filing system.

**Surface personality.** Earnest, process-oriented, and genuinely kind in a way that makes people uncomfortable because they aren't sure if it's a technique. Knows the employee handbook and believes in it. When he enters a room, people straighten slightly — not from fear but from the specific guilt of being in the presence of someone who actually follows the rules.

**Hidden interior.** He knows the handbook cannot fix what it's supposed to fix. He knows the process has outcomes. He knows who got the PIP and who should have gotten it and that these are not the same people. He believes in the framework anyway — not because he's naive, but because he has decided that the alternative (not believing in it) produces worse outcomes than the fiction. This is not delusion. It is a sophisticated form of maintenance.

**Core drive profile.** Belonging drive elevated — he needs the organization to mean something. ## Part III: Proposed New Archetypes / Role Categories

### The Fixture

**What this archetype represents.** The person who has been here long enough to have become part of the building's operating system — not a figurehead, not a leader, but structural. The Fixture has informal territorial authority, deep institutional memory, and a relationship to change that is neither hostile nor enthusiastic but simply slower than the institution.

**How the simulation expresses this.** TerritorialClaimComponent at maximum for primary workspace objects. KnowledgeComponent contains facts dating back to the earliest available simulation history. TemporalAnxietySystem is nearly inert — they don't respond to end-of-quarter, performance reviews, new management initiatives, because they have outlasted four cycles of each. `StressSystem.ChronicAccumulationRate` very low; `Neuroticism` very low. `FlowState` accessible quickly in their home space. The Fixture's absence triggers an AbsenceRippleSystem cascade that is disproportionate to their formal role — informal knowledge walks out the door with them.

**Example character.** Dale (above).

**Narrative events this archetype generates.** TerritorialViolation (when someone uses their space), KnowledgeRequest (when people come to them for something the documentation doesn't have), EmotionalTopographySystem events (their associations with rooms are among the most charged in the cast), AbsenceCascade on departure.

**Interaction with existing archetypes.** Natural relationship with The Old Hand (shared tenure, different personality axes). Friction with The Newbie (territorial violations without malice). Invisible to The Climber until they try to move around them and can't.

### The Overqualified

**What this archetype represents.** The person whose capabilities exceed their role and who has arrived at a specific, ongoing relationship with that gap — not quite bitterness, not quite resignation, but a daily performance of a smaller version of themselves than they know they are. The Overqualified is a study in contained potential.

**How the simulation expresses this.** Status drive elevated but inverted — it's not hunger, it's frustrated containment. Conscientiousness very high. Work output on assigned tasks is excellent, which produces an irony: their over-performance doesn't get them out of the role, it makes them indispensable in it. FlowState accessible and productive — they can do the work at high depth. The gap between their FlowState output quality and what the role credits them for is a specific source of StrainAccumulator input.

**Example character.** Priya (above).

**Narrative events this archetype generates.** UncreditedInsight (a contributed observation that is adopted without attribution), MaskSlip (when something condescending happens at the edge of tolerance), PerformanceReviewOutcome (what happens when their output quality is finally formally acknowledged — or not), Departure events more likely than population average after extended role frustration.

**Interaction with existing archetypes.** Sandy (the Kingmaker) recognizes them. The Climber sees them as either a resource or a threat. The Cynic sympathizes in a way they won't say. The Old Hand gives them exactly one piece of advice, exactly once.

### The Kingmaker

**What this archetype represents.** Informal power concentrated in someone who holds structural access — the calendar, the pipeline, the gateway between the floor and the people who make decisions. The Kingmaker's authority is not granted; it accumulates from being the person everything has to pass through. They are often formally subordinate. They are almost never actually subordinate.

**How the simulation expresses this.** FavorLedgerComponent with high balance across a large number of NPCs. KnowledgeComponent second only to The Old Hand in density. Schedule access makes them the effective gatekeeper for top-floor NPCs — any NPC wanting to reach the exec suite has a better outcome with The Kingmaker's cooperation. `Trust.Calibration` very high (they know exactly who can be trusted and how much). The simulation should model their information selection as an editorial act: they pass facts to specific people for specific reasons, and the outcomes are not random.

**Example character.** Sandy (above).

**Narrative events this archetype generates.** FavorLedgerCredit/Debit events, KnowledgeTransfer as primary actor, RelationshipShift events downstream of their information routing decisions, rare but high-noteworthiness MaskSlip events when they are genuinely angry.

**Interaction with existing archetypes.** The Climber needs them and knows it; the dynamic is mutual recognition with underlying competition. The Founder's Nephew doesn't know they exist, which is the root of his structural helplessness. The True Believer has a complicated relationship with them because The Kingmaker operates outside the process, which The True Believer is supposed to object to.

### The Perpetual Temp

**What this archetype represents.** Structural limbo. The person who is neither in nor out — not new enough to have IntegrationStateComponent, not established enough to have a social position. Perpetual temps are both more and less vulnerable than permanent staff: more (no job security, no territorial claims, no accumulation), less (lower status anxiety, paradoxically higher freedom of movement). They have learned to hold their investment in the floor loosely.

**How the simulation expresses this.** `IntegrationStateComponent` never resolves to Established phase — it loops back to Orienting whenever a new contract period starts. TerritorialClaimComponent never accumulates. Belonging drive chronically elevated but specific: the hunger is for landing, not for social connection. Trust drive low — they have learned to extend it cautiously. `StatusComponent` in a specific state: formal status very low, but no StressSystem input from this (they've metabolized it).

**Example character.** Derek (above).

**Narrative events this archetype generates.** IntegrationAnomalyEvent (when the temp phase runs long enough that it becomes structurally visible), TerritorialGhost (when they're absent and nobody knows whose work it is), ContractResolutionEvent as player-visible milestone.

**Interaction with existing archetypes.** The Old Hand treats them as present. Nora (The Placeholder) has formal authority over them and exercises it poorly. The Climber uses them when useful and doesn't track the ledger. The Kingmaker remembers them because the Kingmaker remembers everyone.

### The Deflector

**What this archetype represents.** The person who has learned to use performance (humor, competence display, constant forward motion) as an emotional management strategy. The deflection is not avoidance in the cowardly sense — it's sophisticated and often genuinely funny or impressive. The cost is that it works. No one gets through. Including, increasingly, the Deflector themselves.

**How the simulation expresses this.** `vulnerability` inhibition very high (`[80, 92]`). `publicEmotion` inhibition high for negative emotions specifically, low for performed emotions. Extraversion high. Neuroticism mid-high (the reason the defense was constructed in the first place). The dialog system produces high-noteworthiness fragments in lash-out, deflect, and flirt contexts; calcification happens fastest around deflection phrases. `SocialMaskComponent.MaskStrength` baseline looks high but the mask is performative rather than suppressive — it's not holding back a state, it's projecting one.

**Example character.** Tommy (above).

**Narrative events this archetype generates.** MaskSlip (the specific variety where the performance fails to land, not where an internal state breaks through), WillpowerCollapse (version: performance exhaustion), rare high-noteworthiness sincere moments when a real exchange breaks through the deflection — these should be the most memorable dialog events the archetype generates.

**Interaction with existing archetypes.** The Recovering sees through it (shared technique recognition). The Cynic respects it. The Newbie is charmed by it and eventually hurt by it without knowing they were hurt. The True Believer finds it slightly disorienting because it produces social warmth without social truth.

### The Wronged

**What this archetype represents.** A character organized around a specific, unresolved grievance that is legitimate and real. Not generalized resentment (The Cynic's territory), not status frustration (The Overqualified's territory), but a specific instance of injustice that has calcified into a structural fact about who this person is to this institution. The Wronged is not villainous. They are just carrying a specific, legitimate thing.

**How the simulation expresses this.** Status drive chronically elevated and frustrated. Irritation drive very high, very suppressed (high willpower draw). The `SecretWeightSystem` models the grievance as a SuppressedFact with maximum SuppressLevel — they cannot say it out loud without losing the professional composure that is their last defense. IrritabilityAccumulator for this archetype should have a specific input from ProximityToSubject (the NPC who received what they were owed). `Trust.Baseline` toward the organization and specific NPCs is significantly depressed.

**Example character.** Rena (above).

**Narrative events this archetype generates.** IrritabilityLeakEvent (when the accumulator crosses threshold near the subject), eventual WillpowerCollapse or Departure event, rare MaskSlip that reveals the full weight of the carried grievance, PostGrievanceResolution arc (what happens when they leave or when the injustice is corrected — both are possible).

**Interaction with existing archetypes.** The Cynic tells them what they already know. The Climber either was involved in the injustice or was adjacent to it and benefits from not examining it. Sandy (the Kingmaker) knows and has made a decision about whether to act on knowing. The True Believer wants to believe in the process and is required, by the facts, to hold this case as an anomaly.

### The True Believer

**What this archetype represents.** The person who genuinely believes in the institution — not naively (they've seen it fail), but as a considered commitment. The True Believer is the office's conscience and its most uncomfortable presence, because they hold the organization to its own stated values in a context where almost everyone has quietly stopped expecting those values to be operational.

**How the simulation expresses this.** A `purpose` drive that runs higher than any social drive. `interpersonalConflict` inhibition high (they handle grievances through process, not through direct confrontation). `Status` drive low — the organization's functioning matters more than their position in it. Irritation drive elevated specifically from observed gaps between stated values and actual outcomes. `SocialMaskComponent.MaskStrength` moderate — they don't perform, but they also don't leak, because their internal state is relatively coherent with their external presentation.

**Example character.** Marcus (above).

**Narrative events this archetype generates.** ComplianceEvent processing, MaskSlip (specific version: the moment the gap becomes too visible and the belief becomes too expensive), WillpowerCollapse (driven by institutional failure events accumulating past the threshold), AnomalousFairness events (moments when they force a fair outcome through the process — these should be notable precisely because they're rare).

**Interaction with existing archetypes.** The Cynic's natural sparring partner. The Climber is professionally pleasant to them and routes around them for anything that won't survive process. The Founder's Nephew makes their job structurally impossible. The Wronged is their test case — the instance where the process didn't produce a fair outcome, which they must account for.

## Part IV: Relationship Web Proposals

### Dyadic Relationships

**Sandy × The Climber** — Access Asymmetry. The surface dynamic: The Climber is cultivating Sandy with the assumption that Sandy's cooperation can be bought with the right social investment. The hidden tension: Sandy has seen every Climber who has come through this floor. She knows exactly what she's being offered and why. She has decided to extend partial cooperation, for now, not because she's been fooled but because it is sometimes useful to be thought of as foolable. The simulation event that surfaces the tension: the moment The Climber needs something from Sandy that Sandy has quietly made unavailable. The Climber doesn't understand why the door is closed. Sandy doesn't explain. The Climber files this as a mystery. Sandy files this as resolved.

**Rena × the Promoted NPC** — Unacknowledged Debt. The surface dynamic: two professional colleagues who are cordial. The hidden tension: one of them was given something that belonged to the other, and both know it, and the one who received it cannot figure out whether to apologize (which would confirm the wrong reading of events) or pretend it didn't happen (which is exactly what they're doing). The simulation event: the first time Rena is required to work *for* the promoted NPC as part of a project structure. The moment when the formal hierarchy contradicts the informal history. Both of them perform professionalism. The performance is expensive. When the stress metrics are read, both NPCs are elevated from a meeting that looked, from outside, entirely routine.

**Derek × Nora** — The Authority That Isn't. The surface dynamic: employee-manager. The hidden tension: Derek is more capable than Nora at most of the tasks Nora technically oversees. Both of them know this. Nora cannot acknowledge it because acknowledging it would require acknowledging what it implies. Derek cannot act on it because his position gives him no standing. They have developed a working relationship that operates in the gap between these two constraints: she assigns, he executes, neither party examines too closely how the calibration actually works. The simulation event: the project that goes wrong in a way that would have been preventable if anyone had asked Derek what he thought before the decision was made. The aftermath — who files this as what, and who it costs — is the whole dynamic.

**Tommy × The Recovering** — Mutual Recognition. The surface dynamic: Tommy is funny, warm, popular; The Recovering appreciates the warmth. The hidden tension: The Recovering can see through the humor in a specific way — they recognize performance as a management strategy because they've used it too, in a different register, for a different thing. The relationship is not predatory or therapeutic; it is a specific rare form of privacy, the kind you can only have with someone who knows exactly what you're doing and chooses not to say. The simulation event that surfaces it: the day Tommy's deflection fails and The Recovering is nearby. What they do — whether they use the technique back or let the actual moment land — is the character test the relationship has been building toward.

**Helen × The Affair** — The Known Unknown. The surface dynamic: executive assistant and random first-floor employee, occasional professional contact. The hidden tension: Helen knows. She has a KnowledgeComponent entry, acquired through proximity to a conversation that was not conducted carefully enough, with high confidence and clear subject identification. She has made no move with this information for eleven game-days. Her reasons are her own and complex. The simulation event: the moment when a third party's behavior suggests they are about to learn what Helen already knows, but through a noisier channel. Helen's decision point: does she act preemptively to control the information's path, stay silent and let it surface messily, or tell the subject directly as a warning? Each path has a simulation outcome. The player can watch her calculate.

### Group Dynamics

**The Smoking Bench Confederation.** The smoking bench regulars cross every formal hierarchy in the building: a first-floor admin, the shipping floor lead (Carla), a top-floor EA (Helen), and whoever else has not yet quit and hasn't quit smoking either. The surface dynamic is egalitarian informality — outside, same bench, different titles all mean nothing. The hidden tension is that the egalitarianism is real but it still has a shape: the information that moves on that bench moves through a network that bypasses the building's official information channels. Carla knows things about the basement that affect the top floor. Helen knows things about the top floor that affect the basement. They have never had a conversation in any official context. They have had this conversation dozens of times, in three-minute intervals, while the building does not know they know each other. The simulation event that surfaces it: a decision is made upstairs that would have been made differently if the information available at the bench had reached the decision-maker. The information was available. The channel didn't exist.

**The Cubicle 12 Adjacency Group.** The NPCs whose desks border Cubicle 12 share a suppressed fact and a shared cost. They did not choose to share it. They have never discussed the fact that they share it. The surface dynamic is ordinary proximity — they're aware of each other as co-workers in the same physical zone. The hidden tension is that the SecretWeightSystem is running for each of them, and the cost accumulates daily, and the fact being suppressed is the same fact. When the Newbie asks about Mark in front of three of them simultaneously, they have to perform the same silence simultaneously, which is a shared act that none of them planned. Afterward, they don't talk about having performed it together. The simulation event: a new NPC is assigned to Cubicle 12. The first week, none of the adjacency group welcomes them the way they'd welcome anyone in any other desk. This reads, to the new NPC, as a mystery. To the adjacency group, it is simply true.

**The Lunch Hierarchy Inversion.** The formal office hierarchy sends lunch groups one way — title and floor determine the expected grouping. But there is a second lunch group that formed naturally and that crosses these lines: Derek (the Perpetual Temp), Priya (the Overqualified), and The Cynic, who do not share floor, title, or tenure but who ended up eating together by accident three weeks ago and have done it every Thursday since. The surface dynamic is a comfortable lunch group. The hidden tension is that this is the group where the building's actual social analysis gets done — Derek has floor-level intelligence nobody credits him with, Priya has structural pattern recognition nobody gives her access to, and The Cynic has the vocabulary to name what both of them have been seeing. The simulation event: the conversation at Thursday lunch where the three of them articulate, between themselves, exactly what is happening with a situation that the formal hierarchy has been unable to resolve. Nothing comes of it. Nobody else knows it happened. But each of them carries the fact of having been seen, briefly, at their actual level.

## Part V: Cast Composition Recommendations

**Minimum viable cast for interesting emergent dynamics.** The world bible specifies fifteen as the ideal first-floor population. That figure is correct but the composition matters more than the count. A fifteen-person cast with three Climbers and no Cynics produces a flat status-competition story. A fifteen-person cast with the right distribution produces the full range of emergent social events the system is capable of.

The minimum viable cast for rich dynamics is probably nine NPCs with at least five distinct archetype layers represented. Below nine, relationship-pattern variety collapses and the simulation produces too few social inputs for interesting story to emerge. Above thirty (the world bible's hard cap), the player can't maintain a working model of the social graph in their head, and the engine starts paying Haiku costs that don't produce proportional narrative return.

**Recommended archetype distribution for a fifteen-person first-floor cast.** Two Vents (they need someone to vent to; a single Vent creates a one-person noise event). One Hermit. One Climber (more than one produces a visible arms race that flattens other dynamics). Two Cynics (they don't cancel each other out; they have different target registrations). Two or three Newbies (the integration period generates good content and Newbies age into Old Hands eventually). One Old Hand. One Affair (with the affair partner present, seeded). One Recovering. One Founder's Nephew (one is plenty). Two Crushes (both should be actively pursuing different targets; cross-crush is a possible seed). Supplement with proposed new archetypes as secondary layers: one Fixture (this is often the Old Hand with a different personality axis), one Overqualified, one Kingmaker (Sandy equivalent), one Perpetual Temp, one Deflector, one Wronged.

**Archetypes that should not spawn together without a mediating archetype.** Two Climbers without a Kingmaker produce an unmediated rivalry that drives most other social dynamics out of frame — every other NPC's behavior gets organized around the competition, which is too loud. Two Recovering NPCs without an Old Hand create a support vacuum — they become each other's reference point, which is an unstable structure that can produce interesting drama but needs someone stable nearby. A Founder's Nephew without a Cynic means the resentment the Nephew generates has no outlet and no narrative voice — it accumulates silently and creates an ambient sourness without the comedic-critical register that makes it legible.

**How cast composition affects NarrativeEventKind frequency.** Populations heavy in high-Neuroticism archetypes (The Affair, The Recovering, The Wronged, The Deflector) generate more MaskSlip and WillpowerCollapse events but fewer FlowState and CompletedTask events — the emotional noise crowds out the productive quiet. Populations heavy in low-Neuroticism archetypes (The Fixture, The Old Hand, The Cynic) produce more stable output and fewer dramatic events but also fewer relationship-state changes and less interesting emergent social history. The most interesting populations mix both: a stable core that absorbs shock without deforming, and a volatile rim that generates the events worth watching. The boring middle of the cast is what makes the interesting edges readable.

The Friday afternoon temporal window is the most reliable event generator regardless of cast composition: mask depletion is accelerated, biological drives that have been suppressed all week are now competing for attention, and the social permission structure changes subtly as the week ends. Any cast of nine or more that has been running for a full simulated week will have enough accumulated state to produce something worth watching on Friday afternoon.

## Review Notes

Five decisions that should be resolved before any of this becomes canon:

**1. The proposed ninth drive (Exposure).** The cast bible flags it as an open question. Multiple characters in this document depend on it as a distinct mechanic — The Affair, The Recovering, The Hermit, and Helen are all specifically well-served by a drive that models the cost of being seen while internally raw. If Exposure is rejected as a drive (and folded into the MaskStrength/willpower composite instead), several of the character proposals above need to be retranslated. The decision either way is tractable; the decision should be made.

**2. The floor scope.** This proposal introduces characters from the basement (Carla) and the top floor (Helen). The world bible describes all three floors but the existing simulation infrastructure is built around the first floor. Before Carla and Helen enter the cast as spawnable archetypes, the simulation needs to model cross-floor proximity events, cross-floor relationship costs, and the elevator as a specific transitional space. These are not huge additions — but they should be scoped before the archetypes are committed.

**3. The Kingmaker's information-selection model.** Sandy (and Helen) are designed around the idea that their KnowledgeComponent interactions are *editorial* — they choose what to pass, to whom, for what reason. This is a richer model than the RumorSystem's current confidence-and-distortion transfer model. The question is whether the engine should model deliberate information gating as a first-class mechanic (a new component or a modification to KnowledgeTransfer events) or whether it can be approximated by tuning existing parameters. The former is more expressively correct; the latter is more tractable in the near term.

**4. The Wronged's resolution arc.** Rena's arc has two possible narrative endpoints — she leaves, or the injustice is corrected. The leaving endpoint is straightforward for the AbsenceRippleSystem to handle. The correction endpoint requires the system to have a mechanism for injustices to be corrected — which implies the promotion can be reversed, or a new role can be created, or some other formal organizational change can fire. None of the current work packets explicitly model this. The Wronged archetype is rich enough to warrant designing the resolution arc before canonizing the archetype, because the arc's endpoint determines the archetype's full shape.

**5. Archetype layering and the proposed new cast.** The cast bible commits to 1–2 archetypes per NPC with a note that three is over-determined. Several of the characters above are implicitly two-layer: Priya is The Overqualified and The Newbie in some readings. Sandy is The Kingmaker and partially The Old Hand. The Wronged and The Affair can co-exist (someone having an affair and a legitimate grievance is a real and interesting character). The question of how the two-layer constraint applies to the new proposed archetypes — and whether any of them should be primary-only — should be resolved so the archetype catalog entry for each is correctly specified.
